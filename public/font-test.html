A first look into Ando

It's Friday night in San Francisco, and engineers are leaving parties early to work with their AI agents. Full attention on the phone, barely pausing to respond. On custom extra-long curved monitors, six agents run across different terminal windows, all in view.

Ando is remarkably quiet online. Though the team has raised double digit millions from blue chip investors, there is no official funding announcement, no public product, no launch party. Sara Du, the CEO of Ando and previous CEO of Alloy Automation, is far more interested in crafting the precise right feeling in the product for early users than trying to brute force her way to more exposure.

Writing this piece feels like divulging a very well kept secret: this small, internet native guild of philosophical engineers are quietly building the scaffolding for a new default communication layer between agents and humans.

San Francisco is a boomtown, a place where the future announces itself first as restlessness and an anxiety of moving too slowly.
